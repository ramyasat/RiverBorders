About River Borders and the author

River Borders is an attempt to create a database of river borders across the world. The points on the map are the labels of the border rivers. Some of these labels have more information about the rivers themselves. There are about a dozen or so rivers: The Amazon; the Rhine; the Volga; Nile; Danube; Inn; Bug; Mura; Senegal; Makona; Zambezi; Congo; Niger; Rio Grande; Colorado; Indus; Mekong; Salween; the Detroit River This is an evolving list. I have used MapBox to create the maps juxtaposing the rivers and river borders. I used QGIS to put together some shapefiles of the rivres.

I am Ramya, a PhD candidate in History at Michigan State. I study river borders, specifically, the Detroit River which forms the border between the Canada and United States. I study the effects of dredging on the river. This website is my attempt to offer a one-stop source for all river borders. I hope educators can use this website to help their students get a sense of river borders across the world, their scale and sheer numbers. If you observe any discrepancies or have additions, please feel free to get in touch.
